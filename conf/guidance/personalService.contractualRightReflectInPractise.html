If the end client is allowed to turn away another suitably qualified person you send to do the work, it's more likely that, in HMRC's view, you should pay tax on payroll. In this case, you're working more like an employee.

If the end client has to accept another suitably qualified person you send to do the work, it's less likely that you should pay tax on payroll. It doesn't have to be you personally who provides the services. In this case, you're working more like a business.
